The Business

What Sutera Sites does, and who it serves.

The Operator

How I work, and what to expect from me.

Sutera Sites is run solo. I (James) handle quoting, builds, ads management, delivery, and client communication. You bring the leads, I close them and deliver the work. Here's how I operate so we don't waste time discovering it on the job.

Direct, no fluff

I'll tell you straight if something's working or not. I expect the same. If you don't know something, ask. If you stuffed up, say so. We move on faster that way.

Fast turnaround

If you bring me a qualified lead, expect a same-day quote 90% of the time. If a client signs on a Friday, they're onboarded by Monday. Speed is part of our edge.

Honesty over politeness

If I think a lead is a bad fit, I'll say so before we quote. If you're not hitting cadence, we'll have a real conversation about why. No passive-aggressive drift.

Treating this like a business

Even though we're friends, this is a paying partnership. Signed agreement, tracked numbers, real performance conversations. The structure protects both of us long-term.

Available, not on-call

I respond to most messages within a few hours during the day. Outside business hours I'll get to it the next morning. Genuine emergencies (client lost site, ads broken): call me.

Quality over volume of clients

I'd rather onboard 5 long-term clients than churn 15. Bring me leads who actually fit our model - owner-operated, in-market for what we sell, can afford the package. Quantity-only leads waste both of our time.

The Role

What you actually do day to day.

You're the outbound sales engine. You prospect, qualify, and hand-off. I handle everything from the quote forward. The cleaner that hand-off, the more closes we get and the more you earn.

Your responsibilities

  • Build and maintain a list of qualified local Melbourne businesses
  • Cold-call, cold-email, walk-in, or LinkedIn-message that list daily
  • Qualify the lead: are they decision-maker, owner-operated, in-market, can afford
  • Book a discovery call between the lead and James
  • Pass the lead to James with notes (who they are, what they want, why they're interested)
  • Follow up on quotes that haven't closed (with James's prompt)
  • Track your own activity (dials, conversations, meetings, closes)

What you do not do

  • Quote clients yourself (James handles all pricing)
  • Promise specific deliverables, timelines, or features
  • Build websites, run ads, or do any delivery work
  • Handle client billing, support, or ongoing comms once onboarded
  • Sign anything on behalf of Sutera Sites
The Commission Structure

How you get paid.

All compensation is recurring, percentage-based, and tied to client monthly recurring revenue (MRR). No base salary. No upfront signing fee. You earn from every dollar a client you brought us actually pays.

40% of MRR · Months 1-6

For the first six months of a client's lifecycle (their lock-in period), you earn 40% of their monthly recurring revenue. This is the heaviest paycheque period.

15% of MRR · Month 7 onwards

From month 7 forward, you continue to earn 15% of MRR for as long as that client remains active with Sutera Sites. The tail is ongoing - there's no roll-off.

Monthly volume bonuses

On top of commission, you earn a one-time bonus each calendar month based on the number of new clients onboarded that month. Resets on the 1st of every month.

Paid 3x per month

Payouts on the 10th, 20th, and 30th of every month. Each payout covers all client payments cleared since the previous payout. Monthly bonus pays on the 10th of the following month.

Activity floor

Three consecutive calendar months with zero new closes drops your ongoing 15% rate to 5% until you bring in one new client. Restores immediately. Keeps the structure honest.

14-day exit

Either of us can end the arrangement with 14 days' notice. You keep your ongoing 15% commission on every client already onboarded. No clawback.

What counts as your client

Counts as yours

A lead you personally identified, contacted, and qualified. A warm intro you facilitated that led to a meeting James took.

Does not count

A name on a list passed to James without qualification. An inbound enquiry that came to Sutera Sites directly during the term.

Grey areas get sorted before James sends the quote, not after the client signs. If there's any ambiguity about whose lead it is, raise it early.

Cancellation rules

  • Client cancels in first 30 days: no commission paid.
  • Client cancels months 2-6 (during lock-in): you keep what was paid up to that point. No further commission accrues.
  • Client cancels month 7+: you keep everything earned. No clawback.

How We Work Together

The day-to-day cadence.

The mechanics of how leads, communication, and accountability flow between us. The cleaner the process, the more closes you bank.

Lead handoff

When you've qualified a lead, you message me with: business name, contact name, what they're interested in, why they're interested, best time to call. I respond same-day with next steps.

Weekly check-in

15-minute call each Monday. Review last week's activity (dials, conversations, meetings, closes), surface any stuck leads, plan the week ahead. Non-negotiable - missing it twice in a row is a red flag.

CRM & tracking

You log every contacted prospect in our shared CRM (notion or a simple spreadsheet to start). Activity tracking is on you - dials, conversations, meetings, status. Visibility = trust.

Monthly commission statement

On the 10th of every month, I share your full statement: clients on your book, MRR per client, months remaining at 40%, ongoing 15%, projected next 3 payouts. Total transparency.

Sales materials

You'll have access to our pitch deck, case studies, email templates, cold-call scripts, and prospect list templates. Use them as a starting point - then refine as you find what works for you.

90-day review

At the 90-day mark we sit down and review: are you hitting cadence, is the structure working for you, what needs to change. Either of us can renegotiate terms in writing at any point.

Realistic expectations for the ramp.

List quality > list volume

200 dials to a refined list outperforms 1,000 dials to a random list. We'll spend the first week building a proper target list together: owner-operated Melbourne service businesses, in the right revenue range, with poor or no websites.

Multi-channel doubles your close rate

Pure cold dialing is the hardest path. The right mix is roughly 50% calls, 30% emails, 20% in-person walk-ins and LinkedIn. For our market, in-person walk-ins convert 5-10x a cold call. Use them.

Plan for parallel income

Month 1 might pay you $500-$1,500 in commission. Month 3 might pay $1,500-$3,500. Keep your current job through that window, scale it down as your cheque grows. Crossover usually happens around month 4-5.

Optional cash-flow draw

If finances are tight during the ramp, I can offer up to $500/month as an interest-free draw for the first 3 months, repaid at 50% per month from later commissions. Talk to me - it's not in the agreement by default.

Who This Works For

Honest about the fit.

This is a high-upside, high-effort role. It rewards people who treat sales as a craft and prospect every day without needing to be chased. It doesn't suit people who want predictable hours or guaranteed income.

This works if you

  • Can handle daily rejection without losing momentum
  • Are self-motivated - no one is watching you dial
  • Like the idea of uncapped income tied to your own effort
  • Want to learn sales as a real career skill
  • Can have honest conversations when numbers aren't there
  • Have flexibility around your current job to ramp this in parallel

This is not the right fit if you

  • Want a guaranteed paycheque from day one
  • Find cold outreach genuinely unbearable rather than just uncomfortable
  • Need someone to set your daily activity for you
  • Take performance feedback personally
  • Can't commit to consistent daily prospecting for 3+ months
  • Are looking for a side-hustle with minimal effort
