The deal

How you get paid.

All your pay is recurring and tied to a percentage of each client's monthly subscription. No base, no signing bonus. You earn off every dollar a client you brought us actually pays.

40% of MRR · Months 1-6

For the first 6 months of a client's life (their lock-in period), you get 40% of what they pay each month. These are the chunky months.

15% of MRR · Month 7 onwards

From month 7 onwards you keep earning 15% for as long as the client stays active. No roll-off. Build a book over time and the tail keeps paying.

Monthly volume bonuses

On top of commission, you get a one-off bonus each calendar month based on how many new clients you onboard. Resets the 1st of every month.

Closes in calendar monthBonus
3 new clients+$100
5 new clients+$250
8 new clients+$500

Paid 3x a month

Payouts on the 10th, 20th, and 30th. Each one covers whatever has cleared in Stripe since the last payout. Monthly bonus lands on the 10th of the next month once the calendar month closes.

Activity floor

Three calendar months in a row with zero closes drops your ongoing 15% to 5% until you bring in one new client. Restores immediately. Keeps you actually doing the job.

14-day exit

Either of us can pull the plug with 14 days' notice. You keep your 15% on every client already on your book. No clawback, no drama.

What counts as your client

Yours

A lead you found, contacted and qualified yourself. A warm intro you set up that led to a call I took.

Not yours

A name you passed without actually qualifying. Anyone who came in through the website or my own network on their own.

Grey areas get sorted before I send the quote, not after. If you're not sure whose lead it is, just say so up front and we'll work it out.

If a client cancels

  • First 30 days: no commission paid.
  • Months 2-6 (during lock-in): you keep what's already been paid out. Nothing further accrues.
  • Month 7+: you keep everything you've earned. No clawback.

How it'll actually work

The day-to-day stuff.

Nothing complicated. You do your thing, I do mine, we sync when it matters.

Passing me a lead

Once you've qualified someone, shoot me a message with: who they are, what they're after, why they're interested, and the best time to call them. I'll move on it same day.

Tracking your numbers

Keep a simple sheet or Notion page with who you've contacted, where they're at in your pipeline, and any follow-ups due. I'll help you set this up if you want - it's how you'll know your own income's coming.

Commission statements

10th of every month I'll send you the full picture: which clients are on your book, what they're paying, how many months until they shift to the tail rate, and what your next payouts look like.

Stuff I'll give you

The pitch info, case studies, cold-call openers, email templates, and a starter prospect list. Tweak it all to suit how you sell. The materials are a starting point not a script.

The first few months

Real talk on the ramp.

The first 2-3 months are a slow burn while you figure out your rhythm and the book starts building. Don't quit your current job yet - keep it ticking over while this ramps.

Plan for parallel income

Month 1 might pay you $500-$1,500. Month 3 maybe $1,500-$3,500. Keep your shifts while it builds. Most people find the crossover with their old wage hits around month 4-5 if they're putting in the work.

We'll build your list together

First week is finding the right businesses to chase - owner-operated, Melbourne, the right size and trade, no current agency. A good list is worth more than dial volume.

Loose pace to aim for

3 closes/month is roughly 15-25 calls a day. 5 closes/month is 25-45/day. 10/month is hard yards - 45-90/day. First month you'll need more per close while you find your pitch.

Cash-flow draw if you need it

If money's tight while you're ramping, I can advance you up to $500/month for the first 3 months as a draw against future commission, repaid at 50%/month from later payouts. Just ask.

Real talk: this only works if you actually do it. Some weeks will be rough, that's just how sales is. If the dials aren't going in, the cheques don't come in. I'll be honest with you if it's drifting, and if it turns out it's not your thing after a few months we'll work it out without it getting weird.
