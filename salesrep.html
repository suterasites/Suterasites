The business

What Sutera Sites does.

Sutera Sites is the Melbourne agency James runs. It builds websites and runs Google Ads for local service businesses - tradies, civil contractors, gyms, clubs, and similar owner-operated SMBs. Four service lines, all of them currently active with paying clients.

Websites

Custom sites either as a monthly subscription (hosting, maintenance, updates rolled in) or as a one-off build. Subscriptions have a 6-month minimum lock-in so cash flow is predictable.

Google Ads

Design & Print

Capability statements, business cards, signage, flyers, quote and invoice templates. Usually bolted on to a website job, sometimes standalone. Quoted individually.

Social Media

Instagram and Facebook management. Currently scoped with one active client (ANSC) while the service is productised. Available as an add-on, not a primary lead service yet.

The full client portfolio is on the public site. Our Work covers real case studies (Apollo, Select Civil, Grout Academy, ANSC). The site at suterasites.com.au is the same pitch any prospect will see.

How James operates

Worth knowing up front.

Sutera Sites runs solo. James handles quoting, builds, ads management, delivery, and client communication. The rep brings the leads, James closes them and delivers the work. Here's how he tends to operate.

Direct, no fluff

James will flag what's working and what's not, and expects the same back. Mistakes are easier to deal with when they're surfaced quickly instead of left to drift.

Fast turnaround

A qualified lead usually gets quoted same day. Clients signed on Friday are typically onboarded by Monday. Speed is part of why deals close.

Treating this like a business

Even informal arrangements run on a signed agreement with tracked numbers. The structure protects both sides - relationships go sideways when money gets unclear.

Available, not on-call

Most daytime messages get a reply within a couple of hours. After-hours messages get answered the next morning. Genuine emergencies (client lost site, ads broken) warrant a phone call.

Quality over quantity

5 long-term clients beat 15 who churn in 3 months. The tail commission rewards the same outcome, so the rep and James are aligned on bringing in good-fit prospects.

Honest reviews

If activity drops or numbers slip, it gets raised - not left to drift. Same applies the other way. Better an awkward 10 minute conversation than a slow falling out.

The deal

How commission works.

All pay is recurring and tied to a percentage of each client's monthly subscription. No base salary, no signing bonus. Commission is earned on every dollar a client actually pays.

40% of MRR · Months 1-6

For the first 6 months of a client's life (the lock-in period), the rep earns 40% of monthly recurring revenue. These are the chunky months.

15% of MRR · Month 7 onwards

From month 7 onwards, ongoing 15% commission continues for as long as the client stays active. No roll-off. The book builds over time and the tail keeps paying.

Monthly volume bonuses

On top of commission, a one-off bonus is paid each calendar month based on how many new clients were onboarded. Resets on the 1st of every month.

Closes in calendar monthBonus
3 new clients+$100
5 new clients+$250
8 new clients+$500

Paid 3x a month

Payouts on the 10th, 20th, and 30th. Each one covers whatever has cleared in Stripe since the last payout. Monthly bonus lands on the 10th of the following month, once the calendar month closes.

Activity floor

Three calendar months in a row with zero closes drops the ongoing 15% to 5% until one new client comes in. Restores immediately on the next close. Keeps the structure honest.

14-day exit

Either party can end the arrangement with 14 days' notice. The rep keeps the ongoing 15% on every client already on the book. No clawback.

What counts as the rep's client

Counts

A lead the rep personally identified, contacted, and qualified. A warm intro the rep facilitated that led to a meeting James took.

Doesn't count

A name passed without actually being qualified. Anyone who came in through the public site or James's existing network independently.

Grey areas get sorted before the quote is sent, not after a client signs. Any ambiguity gets raised early, not later.

If a client cancels

  • First 30 days: no commission paid.
  • Months 2-6 (during lock-in): everything already paid out is kept. Nothing further accrues.
  • Month 7+: everything earned is kept. No clawback.

The day-to-day

How the partnership actually works.

Nothing complicated. The rep does their thing, James does his, sync points happen when it matters.

Passing across a lead

Once qualified, the lead gets handed to James with: who they are, what they're after, why they're interested, and the best time to reach them. James moves on it same day.

Tracking activity

A simple sheet or Notion page covering contacted prospects, pipeline stage, and follow-ups due. James can help set this up at the start. Visibility on the book means visibility on income.

Commission statements

On the 10th of every month, the rep gets a full statement: which clients are on the book, what they pay, months remaining at 40%, ongoing 15% rate, and projected next payouts.

Sales materials

Pitch info, case studies, cold-call openers, email templates, and a starter prospect list are all provided. They're a starting point - the rep refines as they learn what works for them.

The first few months

Honest take on the ramp.

The first 2-3 months are a slow burn while the rhythm gets sorted and the book starts building. Existing income (current job) should stay in place during this window.

Plan for parallel income

Month 1 typically pays $500-$1,500. Month 3 around $1,500-$3,500. Most reps find the crossover point with their previous income lands around month 4-5, assuming consistent activity.

List-building comes first

Week one is spent finding the right businesses to chase - owner-operated, Melbourne-based, in the right revenue range and trade, no current agency. List quality beats dial volume.

Loose pace to aim for

3 closes/month is roughly 10-15 calls a day. 5 closes/month is 15-25/day. 10/month is 30-50/day. First month always takes more dials per close while the pitch is still being refined.

Honest read: this only works with consistent daily activity. Some weeks will be rough - that's the nature of sales. If the dials aren't going in, the cheques don't come in. Any drift gets raised early, and if it turns out the role isn't the right fit after a few months, the structure has an easy exit.
